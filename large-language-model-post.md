### [Large Language Model]


1. What book did you choose, and why?
     
The book I choose was one of my favorite childhood books: Winne-the-Pooh.  

2. What did you ask the LLM to do, or how did you interact with the LLM using the book
you downloaded from Project Gutenberg?

The LLM is used was Co-Pilot and I asked it Identify the book using the URL to the book. I also asked who the author and how many books was were written by the author in the series. I also inquire if the book was based on author children and the character reference based on any of the children’s toys.  I also wanted to know there were any other books based off Winnie-the-Pooh.   Finally, asked for a summary of the book.

3. Were there any limitations or challenges you faced while using the LLM?
   
I didn’t experience any limitation from the LLM in answering my questions.

4. Did the LLM correctly provide the response(s) you expected? What type of LLM patterns did you notice which did or did not align to the way a human might have responded to he same interactions?
   
The responses provided by the LLM we much detailed than I expected. It provided the immediate response to answer the question and continue to provide additional details the characters, the name of the author child and the background stories of child imagination.   To me to be a way to display transparency about how the LLM came to the immediate answer.

I also found the LLM interaction to be human like and the follow up after the answer was trying to be more conversational.  It would offer suggestion of other topics it could answer about the book.

Winnie-the-Pooh, A.A. Milne, https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/67098
